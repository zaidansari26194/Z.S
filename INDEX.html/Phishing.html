PHISHING

Phishing is a deceptive cybercrime where attackers impersonate trustworthy entities to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information.

How phishing works?

• Attackers craft emails, messages, or websites that appear legitimate to lure victims.
• These communications often contain urgent language or enticing offers to manipulate the recipient.
• Victims might be tricked into clicking malicious links or downloading infected attachments that can install malware or redirect to fake websites.
• Fake websites are designed to steal personal and financial data such as passwords, account IDs, or credit card details.

Common types of phishing attacks

Phishing attacks can take various forms, including:
• Email Phishing: Using fraudulent emails that look like they're from legitimate sources.
• Spear Phishing: Highly targeted attacks aimed at specific people or groups.
• Whaling: A type of spear phishing that targets high-ranking individuals.
• Vishing (Voice Phishing): Using phone calls to impersonate trusted entities.
• Smishing (SMS Phishing): Sending deceptive text messages.
• Pharming: Redirecting users to fake websites.
• Clone Phishing: Modifying a previously sent legitimate email to include malicious content.

Identifying phishing attempts

Signs of a phishing attempt include:
• Suspicious sender email addresses.
• Urgent language.
• Unfamiliar links or attachments.
• Mismatched URLs when hovering over links.
• Spelling or grammar errors.

Consequences of falling victim

Falling for a phishing attack can lead to financial loss, identity theft, compromised account security, and for businesses, damage to reputation and potential legal issues.
Preventing phishing attacks

To protect yourself:
• Be cautious of unexpected communications asking for sensitive information.
• Verify the sender's identity through official channels.
• Hover over links to check the destination URL before clicking.
• Keep your software updated.
• Use two-factor authentication.
• Report any suspicious activity.

Staying informed and vigilant can significantly lower your risk of becoming a phishing victim.
